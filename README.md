# Video Production OS

Tools and playbooks for turning a messy client brief into a cinematic results film,
using Canva Pro + AI generation, no traditional editing software.

Built live while producing a real 60 second corporate performance film for a liquor
portfolio seminar (client details removed, method kept).

## Case study: from corporate slideshow to 60 second film

The real project this system was built on, anonymized.

**The brief.** A liquor portfolio client needed a results film for an internal seminar.
The previous year's video was 2 minutes 49 seconds of static slides on a gradient,
too long, too flat, forgotten instantly. The new ask: one minute, premium,
"should not look locally made," plus a 30 second speech backdrop that freezes into
a photo wall behind the speaker.

**The raw material.** A messy PowerPoint with 21 slides of pasted phone photos,
two vertical event videos, nine retail shelf photos over WhatsApp, and half the
numbers missing or duplicated.

**What it became.** A 37 page Canva build: a soft dawn opening, a hard silence,
a macro whisky pour carrying the hero number, a triptych club sprint, three brand
blocks alternating slow and fast, a dark map with territories igniting one by one,
and a team close with a hard cut ending. Every number lands on a drum hit and
holds 1.2 seconds over dimmed footage.

**The production math.** One marketer (not an editor), Canva Pro, exactly 5 AI
video generations on a hard cap, everything else free tools. Missing client inputs
never blocked work, they lived in the film as designed [TO COME] frames, so the
client's first full watch doubled as the input request.

**What made the difference.**
1. Assets renamed by page number (P01_, P06a_) in one flat folder, editing became drag, type duration, next.
2. Effects baked into assets: number overlays exported with their dim layer inside, one drag instead of three steps.
3. The one-drag music sync: move the track's drop onto the hero number page instead of syncing 37 pages.
4. A hard AI budget with an attempt log, the money survives contact with "one more generation."
