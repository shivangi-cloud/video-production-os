# Canva Video Editing: The Fixes You Will Actually Need

Collected live while producing a real 60 second client film.

1. **Overlay replaces the video / lands wrong.** Click the page first, then CLICK the file's thumbnail in Uploads. Never drag a file onto a page that already has a video.
2. **Overlay slides across pages.** It was dropped on the timeline, not the page. Delete it and add it by clicking. Only music lives on the timeline.
3. **Durations misbehave.** Always TYPE them with the clock icon. Never drag page edges. Never press Beat Sync's "Sync now", it stretches deliberate sub-beat pages (like a 0.2s black gap) to full beats.
4. **How long to trim clips.** About 1 second, starting just before the action peaks, so the peak happens during the page.
5. **Music sync without pain.** Don't sync 37 pages. Drag the whole track until its biggest drop lands on your hero number page. One drag is 80% of the sync. Then check only the pages where numbers land, nudging the duration of the page BEFORE each.
6. **The film feels like a PowerPoint.** Exactly one cause: pages staying too long with fades between them. No page over 2s except number holds, zero transitions except the opening dissolves, everything cut on drums, every photo gets Zoom or Breathe.
7. **Manufactured camera angles.** Duplicate a page and scale the image to 130% on the copy. Wide then tight, cut on the beat, reads as two cameras.
8. **The final backdrop for a speaker.** Headline above head height, product at the far edges, center 45-50% empty, 10-15% safe margins (LED screens crop), and deliver the frozen frame as a PNG, an image beats any paused video.
